来GEM是开会的

Posted on 2022-06-21 | In Travel

今天是学术的一天，我怎么感觉这么难得呢hhh

Gkirotov (dumd, Edu, not sure, her handwriting is not discernible), showed great interest in our work and even willing to cite our paper. She has a poster on Thursday and I shall have a look at it.

Lisa, the judger, I didn’t anticipate she would tell me directly, also amazed by the comprehensive work and made several suggestions, such as indicating it’s a case study and the satellite labels more clear, yclicks, etc. It’s overall really good and encouraging and I made a perfect logic, from dawn-dusk asymmetry in the ionosphere and then finding the reason by the magnetosphere and magnetosheath observation, the waves we found in them and finally the reason: KHI by the IMF condition.

Then here came prestigious professors who asked a lot of questions during the meeting. Everyone knows him. So kinda nervous at first, especially when he asked what I thought about the Kepko paper. I was like, god, I read that months ago and how to answer that. He saw my situation and explain the main idea of the paper and directly pointed out his disagreement. I was again, shocked, I just cited one paper and pissed off a professor? Luckily I found the reference part in time and told him here we just didn’t want to be biased and thus mentioned all the possibilities. He looked happy in the end. You think that’s all? Hell not. The funny part is when he was about to leave and I want to know him since he’s soooo famous. I asked his name and he pointed at the third coauthor!!! Oh my!!! Can you imagine how embarrassed I was, Eric Donovan, a very distinguishable person with a shining white beard. People called him Eric in the meeting but never the last name! So glad finally meet him. Speaking of coauthors, saw Mike several times, but don’t have a chance to express my gratitude in person. Will try to make that as possible.

Tom, who I think is a post-doc, also chatted with me and expressed the interest in drift mirror model wave. Hong Zhang, a professor I met in GEM, I introduced to my work. She is less research-related but showed a close bonding with Wen and you, especially congratulations to little Ken, and she is going to have a baby at the end of this year. You guys could have a common topic then, LOL.

The last good comments came from Terry, who is at UCLA and familiar with Boyi, suggesting that at some direction of electron energy flux, the oscillations at the low energy region may be consistent with the Pc5 waves, especially in the perp direction. I haven’t thought about that and think that’s a good idea since we mainly investigated the XY plane. He also asked some other questions in detail (I think he provided the most academic questions today), and I answered them well which satisfied him.

I was about to say the E in the flow channel before beads to Xiantong, who asked the question, but when Longzhi and I met with him, we ended up complaining about how expensive the Boston is…
