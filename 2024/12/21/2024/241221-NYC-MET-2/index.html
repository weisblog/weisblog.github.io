19世纪新古典主义将要结束，浪漫主义兴起

Jean August Dominique Ingres

让-奥古斯特-多米尼克·安格（Jean-Auguste-Dominique Ingres, 1780–1867） 是19世纪法国新古典主义的代表画家之一，他以精致的肖像画和历史画闻名。安格强调绘画中的线条与造型，被认为是新古典主义最后一位伟大的艺术家，同时对浪漫主义和现代艺术产生了深远影响。

成为新古典主义大师 雅克-路易·大卫（Jacques-Louis David） 的学生。

虽然受到大卫的严格学院派训练，安格的艺术风格逐渐形成了更注重线条和古典主义理想美的特点。

  1. 新古典主义大师 安格的作品以严格的线条和古典美为核心，他相信“线条是绘画的灵魂”，强调优雅与比例的完美结合。

  2. 对细节的极致追求 • 他的肖像画展现出卓越的细节描绘能力，人物的服饰、肌肤质感和神态被刻画得栩栩如生。

  3. 优雅的理想化风格

  4. 对女性形体的描绘 • 他以描绘女性的柔美和优雅著称

  5. 历史与神话题材，将古典主义精神注入画作中。

老公 1823

This portrait of Leblanc and that of his wife (19.77.2) were painted in 1823, shortly after Ingres met the couple in Florence. Madame Leblanc had been lady-in-waiting to the Grand Duchess of Tuscany, Napoleon’s sister Elisa Baciocchi; Monsieur Leblanc was her secretary. Ingres described him as “a Frenchman, very rich and also quite generous and good, who has adopted us, to the point of overwhelming us with kindnesses and also with requests for paintings, portraits, etc.” Edgar Degas, who first saw these portraits in 1854, described his acquisition of them in 1896 as “the event of my life as a collector.” The Metropolitan bought them from Degas’s estate sale in 1918.

Theodore gericault

泰奥多尔·热里柯（Théodore Géricault, 1791–1824） 是法国浪漫主义艺术的奠基者之一，以充满戏剧性和情感张力的绘画而著称。他短暂的一生以大胆的艺术实验和对人性悲剧的深刻关注为特点，其作品对浪漫主义运动以及欧洲艺术的发展产生了深远影响。

1.浪漫主义的先驱 • 热里柯强调表现人的情感、痛苦和英雄主义，关注个人在社会和自然中的悲剧性处境。 • 他的画作通过戏剧性的构图和强烈的对比，创造出一种震撼的视觉效果。 2. 动态与张力 • 热里柯的作品充满了动态感和张力，尤其擅长描绘肌肉的力量与运动。 3. 对社会现实的关注 • 他将艺术与社会批判结合，关注普通人的困境，例如灾难、战争以及精神疾病。 4. 自然主义与浪漫主义结合 • 虽然受到浪漫主义的影响，他的作品依然注重细节的真实表现，这使他的艺术风格既有浪漫主义的激情，又有自然主义的严谨。

On July 9, 1818, Gericault hastened to the home of General Henry Letellier in the company of their mutual friend, Louis Bro de Comères. The general’s wife had died the preceding month. Depressed, Letellier wrapped his head in his deceased wife’s scarf, his hand in her handkerchief, and shot himself. The gun was still warm when Gericault arrived. The artist made a drawing on the scene from which he produced this painting for Bro de Comères. A version of this subject is in the Oskar Reinhart Collection, Winterthur, Switzerland.

Simon Denis

Flemish, Antwerp 1755-1813 Naples View on the Quirinal Hill, Rome, 1800

Oil on paper, laid down on canvas

For this rooftop view, Denis set up his easel on the upper story of a palazzo on Rome’s Quirinal Hill. His intention was not merely to depict the urban topography. Facing north by northwest, the view beckoned to be painted in the afternoon to take full advantage of the shadows that heighten the counterpoint between the curve in the cityscape and the dome of the sky. This simple record of a few hours’ work is arguably the most ambitious oil sketch by Denis that has come to light.

泰奥多尔 卢梭（Théodore Rousseau, 1812–1867）

19世纪法国著名风景画家，巴比松画派（Barbizon School）的创始人之一。他以对自然的细腻观察和深刻热爱而著称，为法国风景画注入了浪漫主义和现实主义的元素，是推动户外写生的重要人物。

  1. 浪漫主义与现实主义结合 • 卢梭的作品充满浪漫主义情感，但同时也强调对自然景观的真实描绘。他擅长捕捉大自然的光影变化与气氛。
  2. 户外写生的先驱 • 他强调直接在户外观察自然，捕捉真实的光线和环境，是推动现代风景画发展的重要人物。
  3. 枫丹白露森林的钟爱 • 枫丹白露森林成为卢梭最常表现的主题，他用画笔记录了这片自然景观的各种细节，从茂密的树丛到阳光洒下的清晨。
  4. 色彩与细节的精致处理 • 卢梭的画作展现了对自然纹理的敏锐观察，他通过柔和的色彩和细腻的笔触，使风景画充满生机和诗意。

Rousseau’s technique, which builds up a lively, light-filled surface with delicate touches of translucent glazes, is characteristic of his mature style.


1852

As related by Rousseau’s friend and biographer Alfred Sensier, this painting represents part of the woods at Fontainebleau where the trees-some hundreds of years old-were vulnerable to harvesting, a practice the artist bitterly opposed. Although the composition reflects seventeenth-century Dutch prototypes, its contemporary resonance is elucidated by the poignant contrast between the saplings in the recently cleared opening at the left and the aged specimens at the right. After working on the panel for two years, Rousseau dated it, a rare gesture signifying that he considered it particularly successful.

卡罗鲁斯 杜兰（Carolus-Duran, 1837–1917） 是法国著名的肖像画家及学院派画家。他以其精湛的肖像画技巧和对光影、质感的细致表现而闻名，深受19世纪法国上层社会的喜爱。杜兰的艺术风格融合了传统的学院画法与一些新的表现技巧，他对肖像画的贡献使他成为法国肖像画的杰出代表之一。

1840

This training exercise is thought to have been painted by the young Courbet shortly after his arrival in Paris in 1839, when he studied for several months with baron Charles de Steuben (1788-1856) and then at the Académie Suisse. In an era when themes drawn from antiquity and the Bible stood at the head of an established hierarchy of subjects, the successful rendering of the unclothed male body was a benchmark of an artist’s formation. Few such works from Courbet’s earliest years survive, and the origins of this painting remain obscure.

The artist Mary Cassatt admired this picture as “the finest woman’s portrait Courbet ever did.” What very likely began as a portrait has become emblematic of the independent modern woman. Women riding horseback were still a rare sight, and for a woman to ride unaccompanied by a man was considered scandalous.

1864-1868画了一系列裸女

COURBET AND THE FEMALE NUDE

Gustave Courbet relished popular and commercial success, but he also set himself up for controversy, proclaiming himself the “proudest and most arrogant man in France.” In 1866, after being congratulated by stylistically conservative contemporaries for the success at the Salon of Woman with a Parrot (on display in this gallery), the artist boasted to an old friend, “I told you a long time ago that I would give them a fist right in the face. That bunch of scoundrels, they caught it!” In Courbet’s paintings of female nudes of the 1850s and 1860s, physical details-body hair, a wedding ring—reveal the models to be actual women rather than the idealizations seen in conventional depictions of allegories, goddesses, and odalisques. Courbet was keen to confront the female nude without pretense or prudishness. This candor astonished audience.

1866 below

与主流的对抗

When this painting was shown in the Salon of 1866, some critics censured Courbet’s “lack of taste” as well as his model’s “ungainly” pose and “disheveled hair.” Yet they were not unanimous, and the French state briefly considered purchasing it. Courbet himself wrote: “After twenty-five years of struggle, I am still fighting; and today I am still doing exactly the same kind of painting that in the beginning unleashed the entire official world against me ….” The provocative picture found favor with a younger generation of artists. Manet began his version of the subject (89.21.3) the same year. Cézanne apparently carried a small photograph of the present work in his wallet.

manet的真实和批判

  1. 现实主义与现代主义的结合 • 马奈的作品将传统的历史画与现实主义题材结合起来，他尝试将日常生活中的人物和场景搬入画布，展现现代社会的真实面貌。他的画风既保留了传统画派的技巧，又注入了现代主义的创新精神。

  2. 色彩与光影的运用 • 马奈的色彩使用非常大胆，他常常采用对比鲜明的色调，避免细腻的渐变，直接呈现物体的色彩。他对光影的表现更加强调瞬间的效果，这一点对印象派的画家产生了很大的影响。

  3. 直白的表达与社会批判 • 他的作品往往展现生活中直接而不加修饰的部分，挑战当时的艺术和社会规范。例如，《奥林匹亚》直接表现了裸女的身体，而《草地上的午餐》则反映了19世纪巴黎上层社会的轻浮与不拘礼节。

  4. 人物的描绘通常较为简洁，缺乏复杂的背景和细节，而是通过简洁的线条和明快的色彩来突出人物本身。这种简洁的风格，使得人物的情感和状态得以直接表达。

Odilon Redon

象征主义还有一员

法国象征主义画家和版画家，以其充满幻想、神秘和梦幻色彩的作品而著称。他的艺术风格独特，常通过浓烈的色彩、奇幻的图像以及充满诗意的构图，表达了深邃的内心世界和超现实的主题。雷东的作品往往探索精神世界、梦境和未知的领域，成为象征主义运动的重要代表之一。

浓烈的色彩与柔和的氛围 雷东的色彩运用独特，他常通过深邃、浓烈的色彩来表达情感和氛围。在他的画作中，色彩并非完全反映现实，而是传递情感和精神状态。他的作品往往呈现出柔和的光线和梦幻般的氛围，使人感受到超越物理世界的诗意。

1889

This is one of five pictures of olive orchards that Van Gogh made in November 1889. Painted directly from nature but animated by Seurat-like stippling and stylized passages of broken color, these works responded to recent compositions by Paul Gauguin and Émile Bernard.

“What I’ve done is a rather harsh and coarse realism beside their abstractions,” Van Gogh observed, “but it will nevertheless impart a rustic note, and will smell of the soil.»

1889 Cypresses gained ground in Van Gogh’s work by late June 1889 when he resolved to devote one of his first series in Saint-Rémy to the towering trees. Distinctive for their rich impasto, his exuberant on-the-spot studies include the Met’s close-up vertical view of cypresses and this majestic horizontal composition, which he illustrated in reed-pen drawings sent to his brother on July 2. Van Gogh regarded the present work as one of his “best” summer landscapes and was prompted that September to make two studio renditions: one on the same scale (National Gallery, London) and the other a smaller replica, intended as a gift for his mother and sister (private collection).

Cypresses, 1889, below

First step after millet 1890, below

In fall and winter 1889-90, while a voluntary patient at the asylum in Saint-Rémy, Van Gogh painted twenty-one copies after Millet, an artist he greatly admired. He considered his copies

“translations” akin to a musician’s interpretation of a composer’s work. He let the black-and-white images-whether prints, reproductions, or, as here, a photograph that his brother, Theo, had sent-pose”as a subject,” then he would “improvise color on it.” For this work of January 1890, Van Gogh squared-up a photograph of Millet’s First Steps and transferred it to the canvas.

1890

1890 This still life is not mentioned in Van Gogh’s letters and has puzzled scholars as to its place in his artistic production. The subject enjoys a certain rapport with the mixed bouquets of summer flowers he made in Paris; the quasi-abstract floral wallpaper design in the Berceuse of Arles (1996.435), and the white porcelain vase in the Irises of Saint-Rémy (58.187). However, the palette and style of this painting, especially its distinctive blues and ochers and graphic, brick-shape hatchings, link it firmly with the landscapes made just prior to his death in Auvers on July 29, 1890.
